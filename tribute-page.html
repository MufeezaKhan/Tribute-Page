Mohammad Ali Jinnah

The Quaid-e-Azam, a man with a mission

Here's a timeline of Mohammad Ali Jinnah's life


  • 1876 - Born in Karachi.


  • 1882 - Started learning Gujrati at home and elementary education in primary school.


  • 1883 - He was admitted in Sindh Islamic School in Karachi.


  • 1886 - Migrated from Karachi to Bombay.


  • 1893 - Joined Lincoln Inn.


  • 1895 - Became Barrister at the age of 18.


  • 1900 - Elected as Magistrate.


  • 1902 - Father Jinnah Ponja died.


  • 1905 - Started working for the Dada Bahi Noorrji.


  • 1907 - Became the vise president of The Indian Muslim Association.


  • 1909 - He was nominated for the supreme legislative council election.


  • 1913 - Joined All India Muslim League.


  • 1916 - Presided over the 16th Bombay provincial conference presided over the All India Muslim League Lucknow session; Lucknow Pact signed.


  • 1924 - Presided over the All India Muslim League session in Lahore.


  • 1929 - Rejection of Nehru Report. Quaid presented his famous fourteen points. Quaid and Gandhi met in a meeting and exchanged their views for the future of Hindustan.


  • 1940 - The First talk of a Partition(In an annual meeting of Muslim League held in Lahore. Gandhi strongly disliked this proposal.)


  • 1944 - Jinnah-Gandhi talks.


  • 1945 - Participated in Shimla Conference.


  • 1947 - Partition of India & formation of Pakistan. Became first Governor-General of Pakistan.


  • 1948 - Inaugurated State Bank of Pakistan.


  • 1948 - Died of Tuberculosis.
